## Aspect Ratio Calculator — Find the Ratio of Any Resolution

An aspect ratio describes the proportional relationship between the width and height of an image, video, or screen. It is written as **width:height** — for example, 16:9 means for every 16 units of width, there are 9 units of height. Understanding aspect ratios is essential for video editing, photography, web design, and display selection.

## How to Calculate Aspect Ratio

The aspect ratio is found by dividing both the width and height by their **greatest common divisor (GCD)**:

> **Aspect Ratio = Width ÷ GCD : Height ÷ GCD**

**Example:** A 1920×1080 display:
- GCD of 1920 and 1080 = 120
- 1920 ÷ 120 = 16, 1080 ÷ 120 = 9
- Aspect Ratio = **16:9**

## Why Aspect Ratio Matters

- **Cropping and distortion**: Using the wrong ratio stretches or squashes images. A 4:3 photo forced into a 16:9 frame will either crop important content or add black bars.
- **Responsive design**: Web developers need to maintain ratios across screen sizes. CSS `aspect-ratio` property makes this easier.
- **Video production**: Shooting in the wrong ratio means post-production headaches. Most modern content targets 16:9 or 9:16.
- **Print and photography**: DSLR sensors typically shoot at 3:2 while smartphone cameras often use 4:3 or 16:9.
