## How is the carbon footprint of flights calculated?

The flight carbon footprint calculator helps you find out the environmental impact of your flight. Enter the details of the departure and destination airports into the calculator. The calculator calculates the distance between these two airports using the Haversine formula, which takes into account the curvature of the Earth and gives an accurate distance in kilometers.

Then, the calculator estimates fuel consumption using an average value of about 2.5 liters of fuel per passenger kilometer. This estimate is based on the average fuel consumption of commercial aircraft. The calculator calculates the total fuel consumption during the flight and uses this value to calculate carbon dioxide emissions (CO2).

One liter of aviation fuel produces about 2.5 kilograms of carbon dioxide. The calculator multiplies the fuel consumption by the carbon dioxide production value, so you can find out the total emissions of your flight. With this information, you can understand the environmental impact of flying and make more informed decisions about your travel.
