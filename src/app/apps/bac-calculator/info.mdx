## How does the BAC calculator work?

The calculator uses the **Widmark formula**, which is widely used to estimate blood alcohol concentration (BAC, Blood Alcohol Concentration):

**BAC (‰) = (drinks × 14) ÷ (weight × r) − (0.15 × hours)**

Where:
- **14** = grams of pure alcohol per US standard drink
- **r** = distribution factor (men 0.68 · women 0.55)
- **0.15 ‰/h** = alcohol burn rate in blood (average)

## Why is the result only an estimate?

The Widmark formula gives an **indicative estimate** — actual BAC can deviate by several tens of percent due to individual factors:

- Metabolism rate varies from person to person
- Food significantly slows alcohol absorption
- Medications, fatigue and health status affect
- Drinking pace (fast vs. slow) affects peak BAC

**⚠️ Never drive if you have consumed alcohol.** The only sure way to know your BAC is an official breathalyzer test.
