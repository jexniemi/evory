## Tax Percentage Calculator – Find Your Effective Tax Rate

Your effective tax rate — the actual percentage of income that goes to taxes — is often much lower than your marginal tax bracket. This calculator determines your real tax burden by dividing total taxes paid by your gross income, giving you a clear picture of what you actually keep.

### The Formula

> **Effective Tax Rate = (Total Taxes Paid ÷ Gross Income) × 100**

For example, if your gross salary is $5,000/month and $1,200 is withheld for all taxes, your effective rate is $1,200 ÷ $5,000 × 100 = **24%**.

### Effective vs. Marginal Tax Rate

Understanding the difference between these two rates is critical for financial planning:

| Concept | Definition | Example ($80K Income, Single) |
|---|---|---|
| **Marginal rate** | Tax rate on the *last* dollar earned | 22% (the bracket for $47,151–$100,525) |
| **Effective rate** | Total tax paid ÷ total income | ~14% (because lower brackets are taxed at 10% and 12%) |

Because the US uses a **progressive tax system**, you never pay your marginal rate on all your income. Only income above each bracket threshold is taxed at that bracket's rate.

### Worked Example

A single filer earning **$80,000** gross (2024):

1. Standard deduction: $14,600 → Taxable income: $65,400
2. Federal tax calculation:
   - First $11,600 at 10% = $1,160
   - Next $35,550 at 12% = $4,266
   - Remaining $18,250 at 22% = $4,015
   - **Total federal tax: $9,441**
3. Social Security: $80,000 × 6.2% = $4,960
4. Medicare: $80,000 × 1.45% = $1,160
5. **Total tax burden: $15,561**
6. **Effective tax rate: $15,561 ÷ $80,000 = 19.5%**

Even though this person is "in the 22% bracket," their actual effective rate is only 19.5% including all FICA taxes.

### Tips to Optimize Your Tax Rate

- **Maximize pre-tax deductions** — 401(k), HSA, and FSA contributions lower your taxable income directly
- **Review your W-4 annually** — Adjust withholding after life changes (marriage, children, new job) to avoid overpaying
- **Claim all eligible credits** — Child Tax Credit ($2,000 per child), Earned Income Tax Credit, education credits, and energy credits directly reduce taxes owed
- **Consider itemizing** — If your mortgage interest, state taxes, and charitable donations exceed $14,600 (2024 standard deduction), itemizing saves more
- **Tax-loss harvesting** — Selling losing investments to offset capital gains can reduce your effective rate on investment income
- **Contribute to a Roth IRA** — While contributions are after-tax, qualified withdrawals in retirement are completely tax-free
