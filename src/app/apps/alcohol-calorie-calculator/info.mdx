## How the Alcohol Calorie Calculator Works

Alcoholic drinks contribute calories from two sources: **the alcohol itself** and **the sugars, carbohydrates, and mixers** in the beverage.

### Calories from Pure Alcohol

Ethanol (pure alcohol) contains **7 kilocalories per gram** — more than carbohydrates (4 kcal/g) or protein (4 kcal/g), but less than fat (9 kcal/g).

> **Alcohol calories = Volume (ml) × ABV% × 0.789 (density of ethanol) × 7**

### Standard Drink Definition

A "standard drink" contains 14 grams of pure alcohol (U.S. definition):

- **Beer:** 12 oz (355 ml) at 5% ABV = 14 g alcohol
- **Wine:** 5 oz (148 ml) at 12% ABV = 14 g alcohol
- **Spirits:** 1.5 oz (44 ml) at 40% ABV = 14 g alcohol

## Why Alcohol Calories Are "Empty"

Alcohol calories are often called "empty calories" because they provide virtually no nutrients — no vitamins, minerals, protein, or fiber. Beyond just the calorie count, alcohol also:

- **Slows fat metabolism:** The liver prioritizes metabolizing alcohol over burning fat, temporarily halting fat oxidation
- **Stimulates appetite:** Alcohol reduces inhibitions around food choices and increases appetite, particularly for high-calorie, salty, or fatty foods
- **Disrupts sleep quality:** Even moderate drinking reduces REM sleep, which is linked to worse recovery, higher cortisol, and increased appetite the next day
- **Lowers testosterone:** Even moderate alcohol consumption temporarily reduces testosterone levels, which can impair muscle protein synthesis

## Alcohol and Weight Loss

Alcohol does not automatically cause weight gain, but it makes weight management harder:

1. **Caloric surplus:** A night out with 4–5 drinks can easily add 600–900 kcal on top of normal eating
2. **Poor food choices:** Late-night snacking is common after drinking
3. **Next-day effects:** Hangover-related fatigue and cravings often lead to higher calorie intake the next day
4. **Reduced exercise:** Hangovers typically reduce workout frequency and performance

**Lower-calorie strategies:**
- Choose spirits with zero-calorie mixers (soda water, diet tonic) instead of juice or regular soda
- Opt for light beer over regular or craft beer
- Drink wine instead of cocktails (lower sugar)
- Alternate alcoholic drinks with water
- Avoid creamy, blended, or sugary cocktails
