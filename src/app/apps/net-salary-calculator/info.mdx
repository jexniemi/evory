## Net Salary Calculator – Estimate Your Take-Home Pay

Your net salary — also called take-home pay — is the amount you actually receive after all taxes and deductions are subtracted from your gross paycheck. Understanding the gap between gross and net income is critical for budgeting, comparing job offers, and making smart financial decisions.

### How Net Salary Is Calculated

> **Net Salary = Gross Salary − Federal Tax − State Tax − Social Security − Medicare − Other Deductions**

From every paycheck, multiple deductions are withheld before the money reaches your bank account. Here is a breakdown of each component.

### FICA Taxes (Social Security & Medicare)

Every employee pays **FICA** taxes regardless of filing status:

- **Social Security** — 6.2% on wages up to $168,600 (2024 cap)
- **Medicare** — 1.45% on all wages, plus an additional 0.9% surtax on wages above $200,000

Your employer also pays a matching 6.2% + 1.45%, bringing the total FICA burden to 15.3% — but you only see the employee half on your pay stub.

### Worked Example

A single filer earning **$65,000/year** gross in Texas (no state income tax):

1. **Standard deduction (2024):** $14,600 → Taxable income: $50,400
2. **Federal tax:** ($11,600 × 10%) + ($35,550 × 12%) + ($3,250 × 22%) = $1,160 + $4,266 + $715 = **$6,141**
3. **Social Security:** $65,000 × 6.2% = **$4,030**
4. **Medicare:** $65,000 × 1.45% = **$942.50**
5. **Total deductions:** $6,141 + $4,030 + $942.50 = **$11,113.50**
6. **Net annual salary:** $65,000 − $11,113.50 = **$53,886.50**
7. **Monthly take-home:** $53,886.50 ÷ 12 = **$4,490.54**

Effective tax rate: 17.1% — much lower than the 22% marginal bracket.

### Common Pre-Tax Deductions That Reduce Your Tax Bill

Pre-tax deductions lower your taxable income, effectively increasing your net pay:

- **401(k) contributions** — Up to $23,000 in 2024 ($30,500 if age 50+)
- **Health insurance premiums** — Employer-sponsored plans are typically pre-tax
- **HSA contributions** — Up to $4,150 individual / $8,300 family (2024)
- **FSA contributions** — Up to $3,200 for healthcare expenses (2024)
- **Commuter benefits** — Up to $315/month for transit and parking (2024)

### Tips to Maximize Your Take-Home Pay

1. **Optimize your W-4** — Update your withholding allowances after life changes (marriage, new baby) to avoid overpaying taxes
2. **Max out pre-tax accounts** — 401(k) and HSA contributions directly reduce taxable income
3. **Choose the right filing status** — Married filing jointly often yields lower tax rates
4. **Claim all eligible credits** — The Earned Income Tax Credit, Child Tax Credit, and education credits can reduce your tax liability
5. **Consider a Roth IRA** — While contributions are after-tax, qualified withdrawals in retirement are completely tax-free
