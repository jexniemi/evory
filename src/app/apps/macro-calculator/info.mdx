## Macro Calculator — Daily Calories and Macronutrient Targets

The macro calculator estimates your daily calorie needs and breaks them down into the three macronutrients — protein, carbohydrates, and fat — based on your weight, activity level, and fitness goal. Whether you want to lose fat, build muscle, or maintain your current weight, tracking macros gives you far more control than counting calories alone.

### What Are Macronutrients?

Macronutrients ("macros") are the three types of nutrients that provide energy:

- **Protein** — 4 kcal per gram. Essential for muscle repair, immune function, and enzyme production. Found in meat, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and tofu.
- **Carbohydrates** — 4 kcal per gram. The body's and brain's preferred fuel source. Found in grains, fruits, vegetables, and sugars.
- **Fat** — 9 kcal per gram. Needed for hormone production, vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K), and cell membrane integrity. Found in oils, nuts, avocados, and fatty fish.

### How the Calculator Works

1. **Estimate BMR** — The calculator uses your weight, height, age, and gender to estimate Basal Metabolic Rate.
2. **Apply activity factor** — BMR is multiplied by an activity multiplier to get Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).
3. **Adjust for goal** — Calories are added or subtracted based on your objective.
4. **Split macros** — Calories are divided into protein, carbs, and fat according to evidence-based ratios.

### Activity Level Definitions

- **Sedentary** — Desk job, minimal walking (factor 1.2)
- **Lightly active** — Exercise 1–3 days per week or active commute (factor 1.375)
- **Moderately active** — Exercise 3–5 days per week (factor 1.55)
- **Very active** — Hard exercise 6–7 days per week (factor 1.725)
- **Extremely active** — Physical labor job + daily intense training (factor 1.9)

### Common Macro Mistakes

1. **Not eating enough protein** — Most people undereat protein, especially when cutting calories. Aim for at least 0.7 g per pound of body weight.
2. **Fearing fat** — Dietary fat is essential for hormones and satiety. Going below 20% of calories from fat can disrupt hormone function.
3. **Ignoring fiber** — While technically a carbohydrate, fiber does not significantly contribute to calories and is crucial for digestive health. Aim for 25–35 g per day.
4. **Obsessing over precision** — Hitting your targets within ±10% is perfectly fine. Consistency over weeks matters more than daily perfection.
