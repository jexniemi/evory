## Grade Percentage Calculator – Convert Points to Letter Grade

Wondering what your test score means as a percentage? This grade percentage calculator converts points earned into a percentage, letter grade, and GPA equivalent. Whether you scored 42 out of 50 or 156 out of 200, this tool instantly tells you your standing.

## How to Calculate Grade Percentage

The formula for calculating a grade percentage is straightforward:

> **Grade (%) = (Points Earned ÷ Total Points Possible) × 100**

For example, if you earned 85 points out of 100, your percentage is (85 ÷ 100) × 100 = **85%**, which is a **B** letter grade.

## Understanding GPA

GPA (Grade Point Average) is the standardized way colleges and universities evaluate academic performance. Each letter grade carries a point value:

- **4.0 scale**: Most common in the US. An A = 4.0, B = 3.0, C = 2.0, D = 1.0, F = 0.0
- **Weighted GPA**: AP and honors classes may add 0.5–1.0 extra points to the GPA value
- **Cumulative GPA**: Average of all course GPAs over a semester or your entire academic career

Most universities require a minimum GPA of 2.0 (C average) to remain in good standing. Competitive programs often require 3.0–3.5+.

## Tips for Improving Your Grades

- **Track your grades throughout the semester** — don't wait until finals to check your standing
- **Calculate what you need on the final** — use the percentage formula to determine your target score
- **Understand weighted categories** — homework, quizzes, and exams often carry different weights
- **Ask about extra credit** — some professors offer additional point opportunities
- **Review grading rubrics** — understanding how you're graded helps you focus your effort
