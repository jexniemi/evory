## Vacation Days Calculator – Plan Your Time Off

Paid time off is one of the most valuable benefits in any compensation package, yet millions of American workers leave vacation days unused every year. This calculator helps you track how many days you've used, how many remain, and how to plan your time off strategically throughout the year.

### How PTO Accrual Works

Most US employers use one of two systems for vacation time:

| Accrual Method | How It Works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Annual lump sum** | Full balance available January 1 | 15 days available on day one |
| **Per-pay-period accrual** | Days accumulate each pay period | 0.577 days per biweekly paycheck (= 15/year) |
| **Hours-based accrual** | PTO accrues per hour worked | 0.0577 hours PTO per hour worked |

To calculate your remaining days:

> **Days Remaining = Total Annual PTO − Days Already Used**

For accrual systems:

> **Accrued PTO = Accrual Rate × Pay Periods Worked**

### Strategic Tips for Maximizing Time Off

- **Combine with public holidays** — Take Friday off before a Monday holiday for a 4-day weekend using just 1 PTO day
- **Book early in the year** — Popular weeks (July 4th, Thanksgiving, Christmas) fill up fast at many workplaces
- **Use the "bridge day" strategy** — If a holiday falls on Tuesday or Thursday, take the adjacent day off to create a long weekend
- **Understand your rollover policy** — Some employers have "use it or lose it" policies that forfeit unused days on December 31
- **Negotiate PTO when hired** — PTO is often more negotiable than salary, especially for experienced hires
- **Take shorter, more frequent breaks** — Research shows multiple short vacations reduce burnout as effectively as one long trip

### PTO Payout Laws by State

When you leave a job, whether your employer must pay out unused PTO depends on state law:

| Policy | States |
|---|---|
| **Must pay out unused PTO** | California, Colorado, Illinois, Montana, Nebraska, and others |
| **Pay out if company policy promises it** | Most states — check your employee handbook |
| **No payout required** | Some states allow "use it or lose it" policies |

In California, accrued vacation is considered earned wages and must always be paid out upon termination, regardless of company policy.

### The Cost of Unused Vacation

According to the U.S. Travel Association, Americans left **768 million** vacation days unused in 2018 alone. That's an estimated **$65.5 billion** in lost benefits. Taking your full PTO isn't just good for your health — it's part of your compensation.
