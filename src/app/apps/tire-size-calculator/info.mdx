## How to Read Tire Size Numbers

A tire size like **225/45R17** contains three key measurements:

- **225** — Section width in millimeters (tread width)
- **45** — Aspect ratio (sidewall height as a percentage of width)
- **R17** — Rim diameter in inches (R = radial construction)

> **Overall Diameter = (2 × Sidewall Height) + Rim Diameter**
>
> Sidewall Height = Width × (Aspect Ratio ÷ 100)

For a 225/45R17 tire:
- Sidewall = 225 × 0.45 = 101.25 mm (3.98 inches)
- Diameter = (2 × 101.25) + (17 × 25.4) = 634.3 mm (24.97 inches)

## How Tire Size Affects Your Vehicle

Changing tire size impacts several aspects of vehicle performance:

- **Speedometer accuracy**: Larger tires cause the speedometer to read slower than actual speed; smaller tires cause it to read faster
- **Ground clearance**: Larger diameter tires increase ground clearance; smaller ones decrease it
- **Ride quality**: Taller sidewalls (higher aspect ratio) provide a softer ride; lower profile tires are stiffer
- **Fuel economy**: Larger, heavier tires generally decrease fuel efficiency slightly
- **Acceleration**: Larger tires effectively change the final drive ratio, which can reduce acceleration
- **Handling**: Lower profile tires offer more responsive steering but transmit more road imperfections

## The 3% Rule

Most automotive experts and tire manufacturers recommend keeping tire diameter within **3% of the original**. Going beyond this threshold can:

- Make the speedometer inaccurate enough to affect safety and legal compliance
- Cause rubbing against fenders or suspension components
- Trigger ABS, traction control, or stability control errors
- Void vehicle warranty in some cases
