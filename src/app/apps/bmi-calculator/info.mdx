## What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a commonly used measure that assesses the relationship between body weight and height. It is calculated using the formula:

**BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)²**

For example, a person who is 170 cm tall and weighs 70 kg: BMI = 70 ÷ 1.70² = **24.2** (normal weight).

## Limitations of BMI

BMI is a rough indicator that **does not distinguish**:

- **Muscle mass from fat** — an athlete may be "overweight" according to BMI
- **Fat distribution** — abdominal obesity is a health risk that BMI does not measure
- **Age and gender** — body composition differs in the elderly and women
- **Children and youth** — growth curves are used for children, not BMI

**Body composition analysis** (e.g., InBody measurement) is a more accurate method that measures the proportions of fat and muscle mass separately.

## When to Contact a Professional?

If your BMI is below 18.5 or above 30, it is recommended to discuss with a doctor or nutritionist. BMI is just one measure — health status is always assessed as a whole.
