## Shoe Size Converter — US, UK, EU & CM Shoe Size Chart

Buying shoes online from international brands can be confusing because the US, UK, and European sizing systems all use different scales. This free shoe size converter instantly translates between US, UK, EU, and centimeter sizes for both men and women, so you can shop confidently from any country.

## How Shoe Sizing Systems Differ

Each region uses a different system to measure shoe sizes, and none of them are directly proportional to each other:

- **US sizes** are based on the Brannock Device measurement system, with half sizes offering a more precise fit.
- **UK sizes** are similar to US but offset — a US men's 9 is approximately a UK 8.5.
- **EU sizes** use the Paris point system, where each size equals 6.67mm of foot length.
- **CM (Centimeters)** or Mondopoint measures actual foot length and is used primarily in Asia.

> **EU Size = (Foot Length in cm + 1.5) × 1.5**

## How to Measure Your Foot at Home

1. Place a piece of paper against a wall and stand on it with your heel touching the wall.
2. Mark the tip of your longest toe with a pen.
3. Measure the distance from the edge of the paper to the mark in centimeters.
4. Add 0.5–1 cm for comfort, then use the CM column in the chart above.
5. Measure both feet — most people have one foot slightly larger. Use the larger measurement.

## Tips for Buying Shoes Online

- Always check the brand's specific size chart, as sizing varies between manufacturers.
- Read customer reviews for notes on whether shoes run small or large.
- Order shoes to be tried on in the afternoon, when feet are slightly larger from walking.
- Width matters too — if you have wide feet, look for "W" or "Wide" options.
