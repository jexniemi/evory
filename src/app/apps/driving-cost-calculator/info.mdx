## How is fuel cost calculated?

In the US, fuel efficiency is measured in **miles per gallon (MPG)**. The trip cost is obtained by dividing the distance by your MPG to find gallons used, then multiplying by the gas price.

**Formulas:**

Fuel (gallons) = Distance (miles) ÷ MPG

Cost = Fuel (gallons) × Gas price ($/gal)

Cost per mile = Gas price ($/gal) ÷ MPG

## Comparison of annual costs

Fuel efficiency has a significant impact on annual costs. For example, someone driving **15,000 miles/year** at $3.50/gal:

- **40 MPG** → ~$1,313/year
- **28 MPG** → ~$1,875/year
- **20 MPG** → ~$2,625/year

The difference between a fuel-efficient car and a large SUV can be over **$1,300 per year** in fuel costs alone.

## Tips for reducing fuel economy losses

- **Smooth driving** — aggressive acceleration and hard braking can reduce fuel economy by up to 30%
- **Tire pressure** — underinflated tires can cost 1–2 MPG
- **Anticipatory driving** — look far ahead and coast to decelerate rather than braking hard
- **Air conditioning** — using A/C can reduce fuel economy by 5–25% in city driving
- **Cold weather** — cold starts and winter driving can reduce MPG by 10–20%
