## How the Steps to Calories Calculator Works

This calculator estimates the calories burned from walking a given number of steps using the MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) method — the standard approach used in exercise science research.

### The Formula

> **Calories = MET × Body Weight (kg) × Time (hours)**
>
> **Distance (km) = Steps × 0.000762**
>
> **Time (hours) = Distance (km) ÷ Walking Speed (km/h)**

The average stride length of 76.2 cm (0.762 m) is used, representing an adult of average height. Taller people tend to have longer strides and will cover more ground per step.

### Worked Example

- 10,000 steps at brisk walking pace (MET 3.5), body weight 70 kg
- Distance: 10,000 × 0.000762 = **7.62 km**
- Walking speed approx. 4.8 km/h
- Time: 7.62 ÷ 4.8 = **1.59 hours**
- Calories: 3.5 × 70 × 1.59 = **389 kcal**

## Why 10,000 Steps?

The widely cited goal of **10,000 steps per day** originated from a Japanese pedometer marketing campaign in the 1960s (万歩計, "manpo-kei" — the 10,000-step meter). However, research has since validated its benefits:

- A 2019 study in JAMA Internal Medicine found that women averaging 7,500 steps/day had **significantly lower mortality rates** than those averaging 2,700 steps/day
- Benefits plateau around 7,500–10,000 steps for most health outcomes
- Even 4,000–6,000 steps per day provides significant benefit over sedentary behavior

A 2021 meta-analysis in the Lancet Public Health found that each additional 1,000 steps per day was associated with a **6% lower risk of all-cause mortality**.

## Benefits of Regular Walking

Walking is one of the most accessible forms of physical activity with well-documented health benefits:

- **Cardiovascular health:** Reduces blood pressure, LDL cholesterol, and heart disease risk
- **Weight management:** Combined with a calorie deficit, walking accelerates fat loss
- **Mental health:** Walking reduces symptoms of depression and anxiety; even 10-minute walks improve mood
- **Blood sugar control:** Post-meal walks significantly reduce blood glucose spikes
- **Bone density:** Weight-bearing exercise like walking helps maintain bone mineral density
- **Longevity:** Multiple large-scale studies show strong dose-response relationships between step count and lifespan
