## Fuel Cost Calculator

The fuel cost calculator estimates how much you will spend on gasoline or diesel for any trip or time period. Enter the distance, your vehicle's fuel efficiency, and the current gas price to get an instant cost breakdown.

### How to Calculate Fuel Cost

> **Fuel Needed (gallons) = Distance (miles) ÷ MPG**

> **Trip Cost = Fuel Needed × Price per Gallon**

> **Cost per Mile = Price per Gallon ÷ MPG**

### Worked Example

You drive **60 miles round-trip** to work each day. Your car gets **28 MPG** and gas costs **$3.50/gallon**:

- Daily fuel: 60 ÷ 28 = **2.14 gallons**
- Daily cost: 2.14 × $3.50 = **$7.50**
- Weekly cost (5 days): **$37.50**
- Monthly cost (22 work days): **$165.00**
- Annual commute cost: **$1,957**

### Tips for Saving on Fuel

- **Use GasBuddy or Waze** — These apps show real-time local gas prices so you can find the cheapest station nearby.
- **Fill up early in the week** — Gas prices tend to be lowest on Monday and Tuesday, and highest on Thursday and Friday.
- **Use warehouse clubs** — Costco, Sam's Club, and BJ's typically sell gas 20–30 cents below market price.
- **Pay with cash** — Some stations offer a 5–10 cent discount per gallon for cash vs. credit.
- **Avoid premium if not required** — Most cars run perfectly on regular 87-octane gas. Check your owner's manual before paying extra for premium.
- **Combine errands** — A warmed-up engine is more fuel-efficient. Short, separate trips each with a cold start waste gas.

### Understanding MPG vs. L/100km

The U.S. uses **miles per gallon (MPG)** — higher is better. Most of the world uses **liters per 100 kilometers (L/100km)** — lower is better. To convert:

> **L/100km = 235.2 ÷ MPG**

> **MPG = 235.2 ÷ L/100km**

For example, 30 MPG = 7.84 L/100km.
