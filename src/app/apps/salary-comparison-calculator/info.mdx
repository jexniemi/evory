## How the Salary Comparison Calculator Works

When you are weighing a job offer against your current salary, or comparing two competing offers, it helps to see the numbers laid out clearly. This salary comparison calculator takes two annual salaries and breaks down the difference in dollars and percentage across every pay period — annual, monthly, and weekly. Enter both salaries and instantly know which is higher and by exactly how much.

### Comparison Formula

> **Annual Difference = Salary B − Salary A**
>
> **Percentage Higher = ((Salary B − Salary A) ÷ Salary A) × 100**
>
> **Monthly Difference = Annual Difference ÷ 12**
>
> **Weekly Difference = Annual Difference ÷ 52**

### Worked Example

- **Salary A (current job):** $68,000/year
- **Salary B (new offer):** $82,000/year

Results:
- Annual Difference: $82,000 − $68,000 = **$14,000 more per year**
- Percentage Higher: ($14,000 ÷ $68,000) × 100 = **20.6% higher**
- Monthly Difference: $14,000 ÷ 12 = **$1,167/month more**
- Weekly Difference: $14,000 ÷ 52 = **$269/week more**

A 20% salary jump is substantial. Whether the increase justifies the career change depends on benefits, commute, work-life balance, and growth prospects.

### Cost of Living Adjustments

Salaries in different cities are not directly comparable. A $90,000 salary in Austin, Texas provides far more purchasing power than the same salary in San Francisco or New York City. When comparing offers in different locations, factor in housing costs, state income taxes, and general cost of living. Some tools and websites provide cost-of-living index adjustments to help normalize salaries across cities.

### How Much More Do You Need to Change Jobs?

Research consistently shows that job satisfaction, career growth, manager quality, and work environment matter as much as salary. However, compensation research suggests the following rough benchmarks for a job change to feel financially worthwhile:

- **Same city, similar role:** Typically requires 10–15% more to offset switching costs
- **Same city, promotion role:** 15–25% increase is common and reasonable
- **New city, relocation required:** 20–30% more to offset moving costs and disruption
- **Startup vs. established company:** Higher base or meaningful equity is standard

### Gender Pay Gap Awareness

Studies by the U.S. Census Bureau and Bureau of Labor Statistics consistently find a gender pay gap in most industries. Women earn approximately 82 cents for every dollar earned by men on a full-time basis. Using a salary comparison tool can help identify whether your compensation is fair relative to peers in similar roles.

### Negotiating a Better Offer

When you have competing offers, use them strategically. Share the competing salary (without necessarily revealing the employer) to prompt counter-offers. Hiring managers expect negotiation — most initial offers have a built-in buffer of 5–10%. Be professional, specific, and focus on market data and your value.
