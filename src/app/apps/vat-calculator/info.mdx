## Sales Tax Calculator – Add or Remove Tax From Any Price

Sales tax affects virtually every purchase Americans make, yet the rate changes depending on where you shop. This calculator works in both directions — add sales tax to a pre-tax price, or extract the tax from a total — so you always know exactly what you're paying.

### The Formulas

**Adding tax to a pre-tax price:**

> **Tax Amount = Pre-Tax Price × (Tax Rate ÷ 100)**
>
> **Total = Pre-Tax Price + Tax Amount**

**Removing tax from a tax-inclusive total:**

> **Pre-Tax Price = Total ÷ (1 + Tax Rate ÷ 100)**
>
> **Tax Amount = Total − Pre-Tax Price**

### Worked Example

You're buying a laptop priced at **$999** in Texas (8.25% combined rate):

1. Tax: $999 × 0.0825 = **$82.42**
2. Total: $999 + $82.42 = **$1,081.42**

Going the other direction — a receipt shows **$1,081.42** total in Texas:

1. Pre-tax: $1,081.42 ÷ 1.0825 = **$999.00**
2. Tax paid: $1,081.42 − $999.00 = **$82.42**

### Sales Tax vs. VAT — Key Differences

Most countries outside the US use a Value Added Tax (VAT) instead of sales tax. Here's how they differ:

| Feature | US Sales Tax | VAT (EU, UK, etc.) |
|---|---|---|
| **Collection point** | Final sale only | Every stage of production |
| **Displayed price** | Usually pre-tax | Usually tax-inclusive |
| **Federal vs. local** | State/local rates vary | Nationally uniform |
| **Business recovery** | Sellers remit tax | Businesses reclaim input VAT |
| **Typical rate** | 0 – 10.25% | 15 – 27% |

### Common VAT Rates Worldwide

|---|---|---|

| Japan (consumption tax) | 10% | 8% |
| Canada (GST) | 5% | varies by province |
| Australia (GST) | 10% | 0% (essentials) |

### Tax-Exempt Items

Many states exempt certain essential items from sales tax:

- **Groceries** — Exempt in most states (not restaurants or prepared food)
- **Prescription medications** — Exempt in all states
- **Clothing** — Exempt in some states (PA, NJ, MN, NY under $110)
- **School supplies** — Exempt during back-to-school tax holidays in many states
- **Medical devices** — Often exempt

### Tax Holidays

Many states hold annual **sales tax holidays** — usually in August for back-to-school shopping. During these weekends, qualifying items (clothing, school supplies, computers) are exempt from sales tax. Check your state's department of revenue for dates and item limits.
