## How to Calculate Your Hourly Wage

Converting between hourly, monthly, and annual salary is essential for comparing job offers, negotiating pay, and understanding your true earning rate. The hourly salary calculator does this conversion instantly.

### Conversion Formulas

> **Hourly Wage = Annual Salary ÷ (Weekly Hours × 52)**

> **Hourly Wage = Monthly Salary ÷ (Weekly Hours × 52 ÷ 12)**

> **Annual Salary = Hourly Wage × Weekly Hours × 52**

### Worked Example

You earn a **gross monthly salary of $4,500** and work **40 hours per week**:

- Monthly working hours: 40 × 52 ÷ 12 = **173.33 hours/month**
- Hourly wage: $4,500 ÷ 173.33 = **$25.96/hour**
- Annual salary: $25.96 × 40 × 52 = **$54,000/year**

### Gross vs. Net Hourly Wage

**Gross salary** is your pay before taxes and deductions. **Net salary** is what you actually take home after:

- Federal income tax (10–37% depending on bracket)
- State income tax (0–13.3% depending on state)
- Social Security tax (6.2% up to $168,600 in 2024)
- Medicare tax (1.45%, plus 0.9% surcharge above $200,000)
- Health insurance premiums (if employer-sponsored)
- 401(k) or retirement contributions

When comparing job offers, always compare **gross** rates — that is the figure stated in job postings. Use a [Net Salary Calculator](/apps/net-salary-calculator) to estimate your take-home pay.

### When Is Knowing Your Hourly Rate Useful?

- **Salary negotiations** — Easier to compare different offers when you know the real hourly rate
- **Overtime pay** — Federal law (FLSA) requires 1.5× regular rate for hours over 40 per week
- **Freelance pricing** — Your employed hourly rate is a baseline; freelancers should add 25–40% for self-employment taxes, insurance, and unpaid time off
- **Part-time comparison** — Is a 20 h/week job at $30/hr better than full-time at $22/hr?
