## How is the loan monthly payment calculated?

The calculator uses the annuity method, where the monthly payment remains the same throughout the loan period. In the early stages, a larger part of the installment is interest, and in the later stages, principal repayment.

**Formula:**

Monthly payment = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ − 1]

where P = loan amount, r = monthly interest rate and n = number of months.

## Payback ratio

The payback ratio tells how many times you pay back the loan amount. For example, a ratio of 1.67 means you pay 67% of the loan amount in interest.

The longer the loan term and the higher the interest rate, the higher the ratio — and the more you pay extra.

## Annuity or equal repayment?

- **Annuity:** monthly payment remains the same throughout the loan period. Makes budgeting easier.
- **Equal repayment:** principal repayment is constant, but monthly payment decreases over time as interest decreases. Total interest costs remain lower.

This calculator uses the annuity method, which is the most common in Finland for housing loans.

## Tips for borrowing

- Always compare the **effective annual interest rate** (includes margin + reference rate + costs)
- Shorter loan term = more per month, but significantly less interest
- Consider payment-free period only as needed — interest still accrues
