## Days Until Christmas Countdown

This live countdown shows the exact number of days, hours, minutes, and seconds until Christmas Day — December 25. Whether you are planning holiday travel, budgeting for gifts, or simply feeling festive, this tool keeps you on track.

### How the Countdown Works

The countdown calculates the difference between the current date and time and December 25 of the current (or next) year at midnight local time. If Christmas has already passed this year, the counter automatically targets next year's Christmas.

> **Days Until Christmas = December 25 − Today's Date**

### How Americans Celebrate Christmas

Christmas is the most widely celebrated holiday in the United States, observed by over 90% of Americans according to Pew Research. Common traditions include:

**Gifts and shopping:** Americans spent an estimated $960 billion during the 2023 holiday season (NRF). The average American plans to spend roughly $900 on gifts, food, and decorations.

**Christmas dinner:** Traditional American Christmas meals vary by region but often feature roasted turkey, ham, mashed potatoes, cranberry sauce, and pies. Southern families may serve cornbread dressing, while Italian-American families often celebrate with the Feast of the Seven Fishes on Christmas Eve.

**Decorating:** The National Christmas Tree lighting on the White House Ellipse has been a tradition since 1923. At home, Americans decorate with Christmas trees (real or artificial), string lights, wreaths, and stockings hung by the fireplace.

**Church attendance:** About 50% of Americans attend a Christmas church service, making it one of the two most-attended services of the year (alongside Easter).

**Santa Claus:** The modern American image of Santa Claus was popularized by Coca-Cola advertisements in the 1930s and Thomas Nast's illustrations in Harper's Weekly during the 1860s.

### Holiday Budgeting Tips

- **Set a total budget** — Decide on a fixed amount before you shop and divide it among recipients.
- **Start early** — Spreading purchases from October to December reduces financial strain.
- **Track spending** — Use a spreadsheet or budgeting app to avoid overspending.
- **Take advantage of sales** — Black Friday, Cyber Monday, and Amazon Prime Day offer genuine savings on popular gifts.
- **Consider experience gifts** — Concert tickets, cooking classes, or memberships often cost less and create lasting memories.

### History of December 25

December 25 was officially designated as Christmas by the Roman church in the 4th century AD. The date likely aligned with existing pagan winter solstice celebrations, including the Roman festival of Saturnalia and the Germanic Yule. The Puritans in colonial Massachusetts actually banned Christmas celebrations from 1659 to 1681. Christmas only became a federal holiday in the United States on June 26, 1870.
