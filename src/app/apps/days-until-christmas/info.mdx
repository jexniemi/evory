## How is Finnish Christmas celebrated?

Finnish Christmas is full of traditions and atmosphere. Here are some ways Finns usually celebrate Christmas:

- **Christmas sauna**: For many Finns, Christmas begins with sauna on Christmas Eve. Sauna before Christmas Eve festivities is an old tradition that helps relax and prepare for the celebration.
  
- **Christmas foods**: Christmas dinner is an important part of Finnish Christmas. Typical Christmas foods include ham, casseroles (such as rutabaga casserole, carrot casserole, and potato casserole), rosolli (beetroot salad), herring, gravlax, and various Christmas pastries like gingerbread and cakes.

- **Christmas church**: Many Finns attend Christmas church on Christmas Eve. In church, you can hear the Christmas gospel reading and enjoy Christmas songs.

- **Santa Claus**: In Finland, Santa Claus is believed in, and in many families, Santa visits on Christmas Eve bringing gifts to children.

- **Christmas gifts**: Giving and receiving gifts is an important part of Finnish Christmas. Most families exchange gifts on Christmas Eve.

- **Christmas sermons and traditions**: On Christmas Eve and Christmas Day, it is customary to sit together with family, enjoy good food, and spend time together. Many families also share Christmas sermons and other Christmas traditions, such as singing Christmas songs or reading Christmas stories.

In addition to these traditions, Christmas is celebrated with loved ones, and the atmosphere is warm and cozy. Christmas lighting and decoration are also important parts of creating the festive atmosphere.
