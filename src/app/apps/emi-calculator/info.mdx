## EMI Calculator — Calculate Your Monthly Loan Payment

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan. Each EMI payment covers both the interest and a portion of the principal, so your loan balance decreases with every payment until it reaches zero at the end of the term.

This free EMI calculator works for any type of loan — home loans, car loans, personal loans, student loans, or business loans. Enter the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure to instantly see your monthly payment and total cost.

## EMI Formula

The EMI is calculated using the standard reducing-balance formula:

> **EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ ÷ [(1 + r)ⁿ − 1]**

Where:
- **P** = Principal loan amount
- **r** = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
- **n** = Number of monthly installments

**Example:** A $20,000 loan at 8% annual interest for 48 months:
- r = 8 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.00667
- EMI = 20,000 × 0.00667 × (1.00667)⁴⁸ ÷ [(1.00667)⁴⁸ − 1]
- EMI = **$488.26/month**
- Total payment = $488.26 × 48 = **$23,436**
- Total interest = $23,436 − $20,000 = **$3,436**

## Factors That Affect Your EMI

- **Loan amount**: Higher principal means higher EMI. Consider borrowing only what you need.
- **Interest rate**: Even a 0.5% difference in rate significantly impacts total cost over long terms.
- **Loan tenure**: Longer tenure reduces monthly EMI but increases total interest paid substantially.
- **Prepayments**: Making extra payments toward principal reduces the remaining balance and total interest.

Most banks and financial institutions use the reducing-balance method (standard EMI). Be cautious of lenders advertising low "flat rate" interest — the effective rate is nearly twice the stated rate.

## Tips to Reduce Your EMI Burden

- **Make a larger down payment**: Reduces the principal and therefore the EMI.
- **Choose the right tenure**: Balance between affordable monthly payments and total interest cost.
- **Negotiate the rate**: Compare offers from multiple lenders. Even 0.25% lower can save thousands.
- **Prepay when possible**: Most loans allow extra payments that directly reduce principal.
- **Maintain good credit**: A higher credit score qualifies you for lower interest rates.
