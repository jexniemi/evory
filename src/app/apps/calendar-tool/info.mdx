## How many days are in January?

January has **31 days**. January is the first month of the year and often the coldest. New Year's Day and Epiphany (6.1.) are common holidays.

## How many days are in February?

February has **28 days** or **29 days** in a leap year. February is the shortest month of the year. Valentine's Day is celebrated on February 14.

## How many days are in March?

March has **31 days**. The vernal equinox falls around March 20, when night and day are equal length.

## How many days are in April?

April has **30 days**. Easter often falls in April. Nature awakens and temperatures rise.

## How many days are in May?

May has **31 days**. May Day (1.5.) and Mother's Day are the most significant holidays in May.

## How many days are in June?

June has **30 days**. Midsummer and summer solstice (~21.6.) bring the longest day of the year.

## How many days are in July?

July has **31 days**. July is typically the warmest month in the Northern Hemisphere and a popular vacation month.

## How many days are in August?

August has **31 days**. August marks the return to work and the start of schools for many.

## How many days are in September?

September has **30 days**. The autumnal equinox falls around September 22. Foliage begins in the Northern Hemisphere.

## How many days are in October?

October has **31 days**. Daylight saving time ends on the last Sunday of October.

## How many days are in November?

November has **30 days**. All Saints' Day and Father's Day are holidays in November.

## How many days are in December?

December has **31 days**. Independence Day (6.12.), Christmas, and New Year's Eve end the year.

## Leap Year Rules

A leap year occurs every fourth year, but there are exceptions to the rule:

1. If the year is divisible by **4** → leap year
2. If the year is divisible by **100** → **not** a leap year
3. If the year is divisible by **400** → leap year anyway

For example, the year 2000 was a leap year (divisible by 400), but 1900 was not. The next exception is the year 2100.

## Mnemonic: Knuckle Method

An easy way to remember the lengths of months is to use the knuckles on the back of your hand:

- 🤜 **Knuckle = 31 days**: Jan, Mar, May, Jul, Aug, Oct, Dec
- 👇 **Valley = 30 days** (or 28/29): Feb, Apr, Jun, Sep, Nov

Note that July and August are both 31 days — this is because August was named after Emperor Augustus, and it could not be shorter than July (Julius Caesar).

## Gregorian Calendar

Our current calendar was introduced in 1582 by Pope Gregory XIII. It replaced the Julian calendar, whose simpler leap year rule led to a shift in time over centuries. The Gregorian calendar was adopted in many countries in the 18th century.
