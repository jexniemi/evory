## Car Logos Quiz – Identify Car Brand Logos

The Car Logos Quiz challenges you to recognize the world's most iconic automotive brands purely from their logos. No brand name hints — just the logo itself. With 16 car manufacturers in the pool, the quiz tests visual memory, brand awareness, and automotive knowledge.

### How the quiz works

Each round shows you a car brand logo. You have four answer choices — only one is correct. A correct answer builds your streak and earns bonus points; a wrong answer costs a life. Identify 12 logos correctly before losing 3 lives to win.

### Why car logos are hard to identify

Many car brand logos look deceptively similar — both BMW and Audi use circular shapes, while Hyundai and Honda share an H monogram. Context clues you normally rely on (color, scale, surrounding vehicle) are stripped away in a pure logo identification test. Research in brand recognition shows that without color, logo recognition drops by up to 40%.

### Fun facts about car logos

- **Subaru's six stars** represent the Pleiades star cluster (called "Subaru" in Japanese). Subaru is the Japanese name for that constellation.
- **BMW's roundel** is often said to represent a spinning propeller against a blue sky — a nod to the company's aircraft engine origins.
- **Audi's four rings** represent the four companies that merged to form Auto Union in 1932: Audi, DKW, Horch, and Wanderer.
- **Volkswagen** literally means "people's car" in German — a name given by Adolf Hitler in 1938 for a car affordable by ordinary Germans.
- **Volvo's circle-and-arrow** symbol is the ancient chemical symbol for iron (Mars sign) — chosen because Volvo was backed by the Swedish steel industry.
- **Porsche's crest** features a horse from the coat of arms of Stuttgart, the city where Porsche was founded.

### Tips to ace the quiz

1. Look at the **overall shape** first — circle, ellipse, shield, diamond?
2. Check for **letters** embedded in the logo
3. Think about **national origins** — German brands (BMW, Audi, VW, Mercedes, Porsche) have distinct design traditions
4. The **complexity** often hints at age — older brands tend to have more ornate crests (Porsche, BMW), while newer brands favor minimalism (Tesla, Kia)
