## Calculating GPA in University Studies

In university studies, GPA calculation is based on weighting grades by credits. Each course gives you a grade and a certain number of credits (ECTS), which describe the course's scope. To calculate the GPA, grades are multiplied by credits, these products are summed, and the sum is divided by the sum of all course credits. This gives a weighted average that takes into account the different scopes of courses and their impact on the overall average.

## Calculating GPA in Elementary and High School

In elementary and high school, GPA is usually calculated more simply than in university studies, as grades are not usually weighted by credits. A simple average is calculated by summing all grades and dividing by the number of grades. This average tells about the student's general performance in all subjects.

## Importance and Use of GPA

GPA is an important measure in a student's academic life. In universities, it can affect things like receiving scholarships, further study opportunities, and job applications. In elementary and high school, GPA can affect further study options and even motivation to learn. **A good GPA** is often a sign that the student is committed to their studies and masters the subjects well.

## Improving GPA

If you want to improve your GPA, it is important to focus on weaker subjects and seek additional help if needed. For university students, this may mean closer cooperation with professors and other students, delving into additional materials, and effective time management. In elementary and high school, additional help can come from remedial teaching, study groups, and support from parents or teachers. Well-planned and regular study habits help improve performance and raise GPA.

**The GPA calculator** is a useful tool that helps track progress and plan studies more efficiently in both university and elementary and high school studies.
