## Installment Calculator – Monthly Payment Estimator

The installment calculator helps you determine the monthly payment, total cost, and interest charges for any loan or financed purchase. Whether you are financing a car, appliance, furniture, or personal loan, this tool shows you exactly what you will pay.

### The Amortization Formula

The calculator uses the **standard annuity (amortization) formula** used by banks and lenders:

> **Monthly Payment = (P × r) ÷ (1 − (1 + r)⁻ⁿ)**

Where:
- **P** = Principal (loan amount)
- **r** = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12)
- **n** = Total number of monthly payments

### Worked Example

You finance a **$5,000 laptop and equipment package** at **8.99% APR** for **24 months**:

- Monthly rate: 8.99% ÷ 12 = **0.749%**
- Monthly payment: ($5,000 × 0.00749) ÷ (1 − (1.00749)⁻²⁴) = **$228.42**
- Total paid: $228.42 × 24 = **$5,482.08**
- Total interest: $5,482.08 − $5,000 = **$482.08**

### Common Types of Installment Loans

- **Auto loans** — Typical terms of 36–72 months at 4–8% APR for new cars; 6–12% for used cars
- **Personal loans** — Unsecured loans from banks, credit unions, or online lenders, typically 6–36% APR
- **Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL)** — Affirm, Klarna, and Afterpay offer 0% interest on short terms (4–6 payments), but late fees apply
- **Store financing** — Retailers often offer 0% APR promotional periods (12–24 months), but deferred interest can apply if the balance is not paid in full
- **Medical payment plans** — Many providers offer 0% interest plans; third-party medical credit (CareCredit) charges 14–27% after promo periods

### Tips for Smart Financing

- **Compare APR, not just monthly payments** — A lower monthly payment with a longer term often costs more total.
- **Read the fine print on 0% offers** — Deferred interest means if you miss the payoff deadline, you owe interest on the full original balance retroactively.
- **Make extra payments** — Even an extra $25–$50/month can shave months off your loan and save significant interest.
- **Check your credit score first** — The best rates go to borrowers with scores above 720. A score below 650 may mean rates 5–10 percentage points higher.
- **Negotiate the price, not the payment** — Dealers and salespeople often focus on monthly payment to obscure the total cost. Always negotiate the purchase price first.
