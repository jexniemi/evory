## How the Tax Bracket Calculator Works

The Tax Bracket Calculator estimates your 2024 U.S. federal income tax liability based on your annual gross income and filing status. It applies the standard deduction first to arrive at taxable income, then runs that amount through the seven progressive federal tax brackets to calculate total tax owed, your effective (average) tax rate, your marginal bracket, and your after-tax take-home income.

This tool is ideal for employees, freelancers, and anyone planning their finances who wants a fast, accurate estimate without paying for tax software.

### The Progressive Tax System Explained

The U.S. federal income tax system is **progressive**: you don't pay the same rate on every dollar you earn. Instead, each dollar falls into a bracket and is taxed only at that bracket's rate. A common misconception is that earning more can somehow leave you with less money — that's not how it works. Only the dollars *within* a higher bracket are taxed at the higher rate.

### Worked Example

A **single filer** earning **$75,000** in 2024:

1. **Taxable income:** $75,000 − $14,600 = **$60,400**
2. **10% bracket** (first $11,600): $1,160.00
3. **12% bracket** ($11,601–$47,150): ($47,150 − $11,600) × 12% = $4,266.00
4. **22% bracket** ($47,151–$60,400): ($60,400 − $47,150) × 22% = $2,915.00
5. **Total federal tax:** $1,160 + $4,266 + $2,915 = **$8,341**
6. **Effective rate:** $8,341 ÷ $75,000 = **11.1%**
7. **Marginal bracket:** 22%
8. **After-tax income:** $75,000 − $8,341 = **$66,659**

## Effective Rate vs. Marginal Rate

These two numbers are frequently confused:

- **Marginal tax rate** — the rate that applies to the *last dollar* you earned. This is your "tax bracket." It tells you how much of any additional income you earn will be taxed.
- **Effective tax rate** — your *average* tax rate across all income. This is always lower than your marginal rate because your first dollars are taxed at 10%, your next at 12%, and so on.

Understanding the difference helps with financial planning. For instance, a traditional IRA deduction reduces taxable income at your **marginal rate**, while your overall tax burden is reflected by your **effective rate**.

## Other Taxes Not Included

This calculator covers only **federal income tax**. Your total tax burden includes:

- **State income tax** — ranges from 0% (TX, FL, WA, etc.) to over 13% (CA)
- **Social Security tax** — 6.2% on wages up to $168,600 (2024)
- **Medicare tax** — 1.45% on all wages (+ 0.9% additional Medicare for high earners)
- **Self-employment tax** — 15.3% for freelancers and independent contractors (see the [Freelance Rate Calculator](/apps/freelance-rate-calculator))
- **Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)** — a parallel tax system that may affect higher earners; consult a tax professional if your income exceeds $85,700 (single) or $133,300 (MFJ) for 2024.
