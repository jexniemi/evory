## Square Footage Calculator — Calculate Area and Property Value

Square footage is one of the most important measurements in real estate, construction, and interior design. Whether you are buying a home, planning a renovation, or listing a property for sale, knowing the exact square footage helps you make informed decisions. This calculator quickly converts length and width measurements into total area and estimates property value based on price per square foot.

### How to Calculate Square Footage

The formula is simple:

> **Square footage = Length (ft) × Width (ft)**

For example, a room that is **20 feet long × 15 feet wide** has a total area of **300 square feet**. To convert to square meters, multiply by 0.0929:

> 300 sq ft × 0.0929 = **27.87 sq m**

For irregularly shaped rooms, divide the space into rectangles, calculate each area separately, and add them together.

### Worked Example

A homeowner is measuring a living room that is **20 ft × 15 ft** in a market where homes sell for **$150 per square foot**:

- Total area: 20 × 15 = **300 sq ft**
- Area in square meters: 300 × 0.0929 = **27.87 sq m**
- Estimated value: 300 × $150 = **$45,000** (for that room's proportional share)

### How Square Footage Affects Home Value

Square footage is the primary factor in determining a home's market value. Buyers and appraisers both use **price per square foot** to compare properties in the same neighborhood. A larger home in the same area typically commands a higher total price, but the price per square foot may actually decrease as size increases.

Key considerations:
- **Finished vs. unfinished space** — basements and garages are usually not included in official square footage
- **Usable vs. gross area** — condos and apartments may include shared walls in the gross area
- **Lot size vs. living area** — outdoor space adds value but is measured separately

### How to Measure a Room Accurately

1. **Use a tape measure or laser distance meter** — laser tools are faster and more accurate for large rooms.
2. **Measure the longest wall** for length and the perpendicular wall for width.
3. **For L-shaped rooms**, divide into two rectangles, measure each, and add the areas.
4. **Include closets and alcoves** if they are part of the living space.
5. **Exclude exterior walls** — measure from the interior surface of each wall.
6. **Measure in feet and inches**, then convert inches to decimal feet (e.g., 6 inches = 0.5 ft).

### Uses Beyond Real Estate

Square footage calculations are essential for:

- **Flooring projects** — ordering tile, hardwood, carpet, or laminate
- **Painting estimates** — calculating wall area for paint coverage
- **Landscaping** — measuring lawn, garden, or patio area
- **HVAC sizing** — determining the right air conditioner or furnace capacity
- **Rental agreements** — verifying listed square footage matches actual measurements

Understanding square footage empowers you to compare spaces objectively, negotiate better deals, and plan projects with precision.
