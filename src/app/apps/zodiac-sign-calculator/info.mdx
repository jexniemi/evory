## What Is a Zodiac Sign?

Western astrology divides the year into 12 equal segments called **zodiac signs**, each associated with a range of birth dates, a classical element (Fire, Earth, Air, Water), and a ruling planet. The system originates from ancient Babylonian astronomy and was codified by Greek scholars around the 4th century BCE. Today the signs remain a popular lens for exploring personality, relationships, and life tendencies.

## How Zodiac Signs Are Determined

Your sign is set entirely by your **birth date** — specifically the position of the Sun relative to the ecliptic on the day you were born. The zodiac year begins at the vernal equinox around 20–21 March (Aries). Each sign spans approximately 30°, or about 30 days.

## The Four Elements

Each sign belongs to one of four classical elements, grouping signs with shared temperaments:

- **Fire (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius):** Energetic, enthusiastic, and action-oriented.
- **Earth (Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn):** Practical, grounded, and reliable.
- **Air (Gemini, Libra, Aquarius):** Intellectual, social, and communicative.
- **Water (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces):** Intuitive, emotional, and empathetic.

## What Are Cusp Dates?

The **cusp** is the boundary between two signs, typically spanning two to three days around the transition date (e.g., around April 19–20 for Aries–Taurus). The exact moment the Sun enters a new sign varies slightly by year because the calendar does not perfectly align with the Earth's orbit. If your birthday falls on a cusp, look up the precise Sun-entry time for your birth year to confirm your sign.

## Sun Sign vs. Rising Sign vs. Moon Sign

Your **Sun sign** (what this calculator shows) is the most widely known placement, but a full natal chart includes two other major personal signs:

- **Moon sign:** Determined by the Moon's position at birth. Reflects emotional nature and inner world.
- **Rising sign (Ascendant):** The zodiac sign rising on the eastern horizon at the exact birth time and location. Reflects outward personality and first impressions.

For a complete astrological profile, you need your exact birth time and location to calculate the Moon sign (which changes every ~2.5 days) and the Rising sign (which changes every ~2 hours).
