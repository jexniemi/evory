## Name Day Search – Find Your Name Day

A name day is a cultural tradition where each calendar day is linked to one or more given names. Similar to a birthday, your name day is a cause for celebration — and in many countries it is marked with gifts, greetings, and gatherings. Use this tool to search for any name's assigned date or browse the calendar to see which names are celebrated today.

### What Is a Name Day?

The name day tradition dates back centuries to the Christian calendar of saints' feast days. Each saint was assigned a memorial day, and people given that saint's name would celebrate on that date. Over time, secular versions of the calendar emerged in many countries, expanding beyond religious names to include modern given names as well.

Today, name day calendars are maintained by official bodies and updated periodically to reflect naming trends. The Finnish name day calendar, for example, is managed by the Almanac Office at the University of Helsinki and contains over **800 names**.

### How Are Name Days Celebrated?

Name day celebrations vary by culture but commonly include:

- **Greetings and congratulations** — Friends, family, and coworkers send warm wishes by text, phone, or card
- **Flowers and small gifts** — In many countries, name day gifts are even more expected than birthday gifts
- **Coffee and cake** — In Finland and Sweden, it is customary to serve coffee and pastries at the office or at home
- **Social gatherings** — In Greece, the person celebrating their name day often hosts an open house where visitors drop in
- **Social media posts** — Modern name day wishes have largely moved online, where calendars automatically remind friends

In Finland, name days appear on official wall calendars and are announced on national radio and television every morning. It is considered polite to congratulate someone on their name day, especially at workplaces.

### Name Days vs. Birthdays

| | Name Day | Birthday |
|---|---|---|
| **Date** | Fixed calendar date linked to the name | Date of birth, personal to each individual |
| **Celebration** | Often smaller — greetings, coffee, small gifts | Usually larger — parties, dinners, significant gifts |
| **Shared with others** | Everyone with the same name celebrates together | Unique to you |
| **Predictability** | Easy to remember — same date every year, printed on calendars | Must remember each person's specific date |
| **Cultural significance** | Declining in some countries, very strong in others | Universally celebrated worldwide |

### Name Days and Naming Culture

In countries with strong name day traditions, the calendar can influence baby naming decisions. Parents may choose a name partly based on whether they like the associated name day date. In Finland, a first name must be approved by the Digital and Population Data Services Agency, and appearing on the name day calendar often helps a name gain official acceptance.

### Did You Know?

- Greece celebrates name days more enthusiastically than birthdays — it is common for the person celebrating to **host** the party and treat their guests
- In Hungary, name days are public holidays if they fall on a saint's day — **István's Day** (August 20) is a national holiday
- Sweden added **gender-neutral names** to its official name day calendar in 2022
- The Catholic Church recognizes over **10,000 saints**, but most national calendars include only 365–800 names
