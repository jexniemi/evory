## How Much Sleep Do You Need?

Adults typically need **7–9 hours** of sleep per night. Sleep requirements vary depending on several factors:

- **Age**: Younger adults generally need more sleep than older adults
- **Exercise**: Intense workouts increase recovery and sleep requirements
- **Stress**: High stress reduces sleep quality and increases the need for rest
- **Health**: Sleep needs increase during and after illness

## Exercise and Sleep

Physical activity directly affects how much sleep your body needs:
- **Light exercise** (walking, stretching): +0.5 hrs
- **Moderate exercise** (jogging, cycling): +1 hr
- **Intense training** (weightlifting, HIIT): +1.5 hrs or more

## Why Sleep Matters

Getting enough sleep improves physical recovery, cognitive function, immune health, and mood. Sleep deprivation is linked to increased anxiety, reduced focus, and a weakened immune system.
