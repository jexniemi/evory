## Sleep Cycle Calculator – Wake Up Refreshed at the Right Time

The sleep cycle calculator helps you find the optimal bedtime or wake-up time based on human natural sleep cycles. Proper timing can make the difference between heavy waking and a fresh and energetic morning.

### What Are Sleep Cycles?

Human sleep consists of repeating cycles, each lasting about **90 minutes**. During one cycle, sleep varies from light sleep to deep sleep and REM sleep (rapid eye movement). An optimal night's sleep includes 4–6 such cycles.

Waking up during deep sleep causes so-called **sleep inertia** – that heavy feeling when you wake up completely exhausted even though you've slept long. Waking up during light sleep phase (at the end of the cycle) is much more pleasant.

### Sleep Onset Latency

The calculator takes into account the average **15-minute sleep onset latency** – the time from bed to actual falling asleep. This corresponds to international research data, which you can explore for example on the [Sleep Foundation website](https://www.sleepfoundation.org/how-sleep-works/sleep-cycle).

### Tips for Better Sleep

- Try to sleep and wake up at the same time every day
- Avoid blue light (phones, computers) 1–2 hours before bedtime
- Keep the bedroom cool (approx. 18–20 °C)
- Coffee's effect lasts 5–6 hours – avoid coffee in the late afternoon
