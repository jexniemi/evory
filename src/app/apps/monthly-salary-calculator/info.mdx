## Monthly Salary Calculator – Convert Hourly Wage to Monthly Income

Knowing your monthly salary is essential for budgeting, applying for loans, and comparing job offers. Whether you earn an hourly wage, a weekly paycheck, or an annual salary, this calculator converts any pay rate into a clear monthly figure so you can plan your finances with confidence.

### The Conversion Formula

> **Monthly Salary = Hourly Wage × Weekly Hours × 52 ÷ 12**

The formula works because there are 52 work weeks in a year and 12 months. A standard full-time schedule in the United States is **40 hours per week**, which equals approximately **173.33 hours per month**.

### Worked Example

Suppose you earn **$22 per hour** and work a standard 40-hour week:

1. Weekly gross pay: $22 × 40 = **$880**
2. Annual gross pay: $880 × 52 = **$45,760**
3. Monthly gross pay: $45,760 ÷ 12 = **$3,813.33**

If you work overtime (e.g., 5 extra hours per week at 1.5× rate), add $22 × 1.5 × 5 × 52 ÷ 12 = **$715/month** of overtime pay.

### When Is This Calculator Useful?

- **Switching from hourly to salaried** — Compare what a job offer really pays on a monthly basis
- **Loan applications** — Banks and mortgage lenders ask for monthly income, not hourly rate
- **Budgeting** — The 50/30/20 rule works best with a clear monthly number: 50% for needs, 30% for wants, 20% for savings
- **Part-time work** — See how reduced hours impact your monthly take-home pay
- **Freelance income** — Estimate monthly revenue based on billable hours

### Gross vs. Net Monthly Salary

Your **gross monthly salary** is total compensation before deductions. Your **net monthly salary** (take-home pay) is what you actually receive after:

- **Federal income tax** — 10% to 37% depending on income bracket
- **Social Security** — 6.2% of wages up to $168,600 (2024)
- **Medicare** — 1.45% of all wages (plus 0.9% surtax above $200,000)
- **State income tax** — 0% (Texas, Florida, etc.) to 13.3% (California)
- **Other deductions** — 401(k) contributions, health insurance premiums, union dues

For a $4,000 gross monthly salary, typical net pay ranges from **$3,000 to $3,400** depending on your state and filing status.
