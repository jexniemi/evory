## Finnish Student Loan Deduction – Overview

The student loan deduction (*opintolainahyvitys*) is a benefit available to students in Finland who take out a government-guaranteed study loan. Its purpose is to encourage students to graduate on time and to ease loan repayment after finishing their studies.

To qualify, the student must graduate within the target time for their degree. The target time varies by degree level and program.

The deduction is not applied automatically — you must apply through the online service or submit a form to Kela (Social Insurance Institution of Finland). Note that the deduction is taxable income and must be declared in your tax return.

## How the Deduction Is Calculated

The deduction is based on both the total loan amount and the scope of the degree. The loan must be at least €2,500 to qualify. Up to 40% of the amount exceeding €2,500 can be deducted, subject to maximum limits determined by degree scope. Check the current maximum amounts on the [Kela website](https://www.kela.fi).

**Student loan deduction = (Loan taken (or max considered amount by degree scope) − €2,500) × 0.40**

| Degree scope | Max considered loan |
|---|---|
| 180 credits | €10,800 |
| 210 credits | €12,800 |

If the loan is less than €2,500 or the degree is under 180 credits, no deduction is granted.
