## Currency Converter — Convert Currencies Instantly

The currency converter lets you convert over 20 major world currencies with indicative exchange rates. Whether you are planning a vacation, shopping from an international website, or comparing overseas prices, this tool gives you a fast estimate.

### How Currency Conversion Works

Every currency pair has an **exchange rate** that tells you how much one unit of the first currency is worth in the second. For example, if the EUR/USD rate is 1.08, then 1 euro = 1.08 US dollars.

> **Converted amount = original amount × exchange rate**

Exchange rates fluctuate constantly based on supply and demand, interest rates, inflation, geopolitical events, and market sentiment. The rates in this tool are **indicative** — actual rates from banks and exchange services will include a spread or commission.

### Understanding Exchange Rate Types

- **Mid-market rate (interbank rate)** — The "real" rate at which banks trade with each other. This is the rate shown by Google, XE, and this converter.
- **Buy rate** — The rate a bank gives you when you sell foreign currency (always lower than mid-market).
- **Sell rate** — The rate a bank charges when you buy foreign currency (always higher than mid-market).
- **Spread** — The difference between buy and sell rates, which is how exchange services make money. Airport kiosks typically have the widest spreads (3–10%).

### Tips for Getting the Best Exchange Rate

1. **Avoid airport currency exchanges** — They charge the highest margins (up to 10%).
2. **Use your debit card at local ATMs** — Usually offers near-interbank rates plus a small fee.
3. **Pay in local currency** — When given the option of "pay in your home currency" (Dynamic Currency Conversion), always decline. The merchant's rate is almost always worse.
4. **Compare online services** — Services like Wise (TransferWise), Revolut, and OFX often offer rates closer to mid-market than traditional banks.
5. **Order currency from your bank in advance** — Better rates than last-minute airport exchanges.
6. **Watch for zero-commission traps** — "No commission" services often hide costs in a poor exchange rate.
